    We're finally starting up a small digital humanities initiative at UT, Knoxville. One of the three elements I'm proposing to the Office of Research and to the College is, of course, a speaker/workshop series. Outside of my twitter community and watching announcements from centers on their speakers series, it would be cool to have a central place to identify people willing to talk or run a workshop on their area of expertise/passion. Does anything like this exist? Would it be an appropriate thing for DHAnswers?

  3. Bethany Nowviskie
    Admin

    I like St√©fan's suggestion, not only because I agree about over-extending the DHanswers framework (though it's fantastic that the site is becoming the community focal point we thought it could be!), but also because I'd worry that a blanket "I'm generally available to speak on X" wiki would be hard to maintain, and that the best speakers and workshop leaders might actually be reluctant to advertise themselves in this way!

    It does seem much better to me to use DHanswers to ask specific questions ("Who is known as an authority on X? Who gives great presentations on Y?") that will, I'm sure, get an immediate, crowdsourced response!

    I agree w/ St√©fan & Bethany here but just want to note that a speaker wiki is something that at least Back in the Day was used widely in the valley to increase opportunities for women to speak on tech-related issues (and management, and wellness, and marketing, and a whole host of other things) and there weren't a lot of problems with people adding or updating their information. I understand where you're coming from, Bethany, about people being reluctant, but to my mind, if someone is out there as an available speaker, and likes that, and has the bandwidth to spare it, they would have the motivation to maintain their listing.

    All that is to say that if a speaker wiki pops up, I'd most certainly put my name on it, and maintain my listing as appropriate, because I'm just wired to that way. I can't be the only one!
