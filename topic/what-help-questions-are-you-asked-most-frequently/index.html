    If your DH program provides drop-in help or consultation services to scholars, what questions are you asked most frequently? I ask because we're working on providing these services and, though we know we'll be surprised no matter what, we'd like to be prepared for these questions with some go-to tools.

    Based on conversations with our faculty and students, I've compiled the following list. What else should I have on my radar?

    (For the time being, I'm excluding questions about how to publish a website, though I know that will be huge.)

    1. I have an Excel spreadsheet of data and I want to visualize it somehow.
    2. I want to create a timeline.
    3. I want to map a bunch of locations.
    4. I want to create an e-book to read on my iPad.
    5. I want to create a multimedia book to read on my iPad.
    6. I want to create a 3D rendering of an old building.
    7. I want to create a keyword-searchable collection of old letters (or manuscripts, or whatever).
    8. I want to map locations, but on a historic map.
    9. I want to create an interactive map for iOS, with information tied to locations.
    10. I want to create a podcast.
    11. I want to create a video podcast.
    12. I want to put my presentation on the Web.
    13. I want to put my presentation on the Web, with audio.
    14. I want to create a wiki.

  3. Patrick Murray-John

    18A. How can I break copyright without being caught?
    19. I have a 10 year old Access database. Should I update it, and to what and how?
    20. How can I move my PageMaker website to a new domain?
    21. I want to digitize and/or catalog my collection of [slides, CDs, DVDs, photographs . . . ]
    22. What grants should I apply for for my project?
    23. How can I build a web site for my course with X, Y, Z pedagogical goals?

  10. Karin Dalziel
    Member

    36. Specific programming questions - how can I do X with Y language? - This question usually comes from grad students or early career scholars who have jumped into learning how to code but get stuck.
    37. Encoding questions - what programs to use, where to start, how to handle special characters, etc.
    38. Where's the bathroom/ref desk/dean's office? <- most common
    39. What makes a design "good?" (This question is usually a lead in for general design help/consultation)
    40. I have a bunch of images, how can I resize them?
    41. I'm going to an archive and need to take pictures of documents, do you have any advice on how to get good pictures? What camera do I need?
    42. I went to an archive and took pictures of documents and they look horrible, how can I fix them?
