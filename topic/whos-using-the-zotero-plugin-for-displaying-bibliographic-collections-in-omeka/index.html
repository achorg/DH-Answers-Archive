    Using Omeka for discovery of bibliographic collections was discussed in a related post by Susan Hollis Merritt (http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/topic/most-effective-software-for-building-searchable-digital-bibliographic-database#post-1856). I'd like to focus on the public display aspect touched upon therein.

    A few of us here at Indiana University are exploring options for fostering image-based bibliographic collections. Most of these to date are driven by scholars so they serve very specific research questions, but our Rare Books library could very well benefit from such a platform for documents with broader appeal. Poking around the Omeka forums and the interwebs, I noticed a lot of interest in the Zotero plugin for Omeka, but I am curious to see some examples of Zotero-based collections using Omeka as an end-user delivery system.

    Please share links and any other pertinent info! I am especially curious how well importing file attachments work. Thanks!
