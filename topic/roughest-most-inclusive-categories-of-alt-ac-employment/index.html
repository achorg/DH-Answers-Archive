    I'm editing the forthcoming "#alt-academy" site at MediaCommons, which -- in a nutshell -- will include clusters of essays on alternative academic careers for humanities scholars and take a bottom-up, publish-then-filter approach to welcoming new content.

    We're modeling the site after MediaCommons' successful project, "The New Everyday." Like that site, we could, if it's useful, include a little field in people's profiles for indicating something about their professional identities. TNE uses categories like "Graduate Student, Assistant Professor, Associate Professor, Librarian, Administrator/Staff" at Institution X.

    In ways, these are useful, at-a-glance labels for getting a sense of who is contributing to the site. For an #alt-ac project, however -- which is all about hybrid professional status and the variety of employment options for people who willfully transcend categories -- labels stink!

    I can't list all options. And I don't want to list so few, or such rigid ones that everybody feels alienated and opts out, picks the same catch-all category ("hybrid" or "#alt-ac professional?"), or reluctantly selects a label that doesn't speak to them at all.

    Then again, I'm the girl who writes down a different profession every time she goes through Customs. Help!

    Please suggest some decent and useful categories of #alt-accers, or convince me to do away with this feature entirely.

  7. Bethany Nowviskie
    Admin

    Thanks, everybody! I'll experiment with a free-text field, which I'd much prefer personally. The way this text ripples through the various templates of the site means that people will need to be fairly concise in what they say. Things are set up by default to support "name, role/title at institution or org." That kind of re-use (in bylines, etc.) was what prevented me from doing multiple check-boxes in the first place. There was also some sense among the MediaCommons editors that there's value in gathering, across all our projects, rough demographics as to commenters and contributors -- but not, in this rather special case, if it results in pigeon-holing.

    (Just as I was writing this, I received a query from a grad student on whose dissertation committee I'm serving. How should I be listed on her title page, in a field not designed for anything other than "Assistant/Associate/(full) Professor?" These small matters of professional identity really get at the heart of the #alt-ac project!)
