  6. sebastian.rahtz@gmail.com

    If you want to make ePub books from existing digital content, the Text Encoding Initiative is always there to help. Making ePub books from TEI is easy (several people have written setups, including me). Adding video and audio is a little more troublesome, as they are not formally part of ePub at the moment. You need to think carefully about your target audience before going down that root.
    I enjoy reading the blog at http://www.pigsgourdsandwikis.com/ on this subject.

    ebooks != epub, of course.

  9. sebastian.rahtz@gmail.com

    What I meant by target audience is that if you are producing content for eg iPad and iPhone, you can safely use video and audio in epub, confident that your readers will enjoy them. If you are targetting any ebook reader in the world (eg inc. Kindle) then you'd better be very conservative indeed in the facilities you use.

    Another route is Apple's iWork Pages, by the way, supposedly has ePub export (never tried it).

    BTW I know InDesign is not cheap, and is complex, but actually it probably only costs a day's salary or less. So the investment is not that great. Personally, I'd always prefer to edit XML and go from there but I realize I am in a minority :-}

  15. Wally Grotophorst

    One more suggestion (Mac only)

    Over the past week or so I've been using Scrivener 2.0 for Mac. It has the ability to export .epub as well as .mobi (for Kindle) files. I haven't tried epub as I'm a Kindle user but I can tell you Scrivener does a very nice job producing multimedia (e.g., text and images) documents for Kindle. You have to go to Amazon and download their free kindlegen utility (Scrivener walks you through that one-time process) and then it's really as simple as click and publish to any number of formats.

    RTF, PDF, Word (doc,docx), Open Office (.odt), ePub, Kindle (.mobi), html, plain text and few others (e.g., multimarkdown)
