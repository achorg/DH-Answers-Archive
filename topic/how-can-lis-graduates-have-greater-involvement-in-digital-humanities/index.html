  2. Arno Bosse

    Ben Showers posted a nice article about this recently which takes a fair shot at providing some obvious areas of overlap and convergence: http://infteam.jiscinvolve.org/wp/2012/02/23/does-the-library-have-a-role-to-play-in-the-digital-humanities/

    My personal sense is that while there are several libraries housing innovative, interesting programs engaging (and actively helping to define) the digital humanities (e.g. the Scholar's Lab at UVA; the NYPL) there are also academic & research libraries that are either flailing (e.g. don't really know how to engage with their departments, students & faculty on DH projects) or are just, well, simply wrong about the strategic approach they are taking. The national, institutional and personel contexts (i.e. convictions of the senior staff) are so varied that it's very hard to draw a general picture. I'd hate to have to generalize. And on top of all that both areas are experiencing rapid change due to various internal and external pressures.

    Perhaps the best way to answer the question is in fact exactly as you proposed - to seek out & highlight for yourself a wide variety of DH projects which you feel are interesting, innovative and meaningful and which you'd like to be involved as well some day, and then look carefully at who was involved and why. Then use this as a guide to chart the course of your own studies and work experience.

    Peter - this topic comes up frequently at THATCamps, and is one that I've been asking now for a while also. My two cents? LIS grads (librarians) need to step up to the plate and become "digital humanists" by doing things that DHers do; write prolifically, build things, have good ideas and implement them, apply for and win grants, and most importantly find ways to connect scholarship to technology. I agree with Arno that there are some great things being done in some libraries, but on the whole, we haven't yet made a clear case that we want to be involved, have the skills (scholars chops as well as technical) to contribute to a project, and that the library is supportive of this sort of work.

    I proposed this as a session at THATCamp Southeast a few weeks ago too. My session proposal is here - http://southeast2012.thatcamp.org/03/08/alt-lis/

    Lets work together to become the "collection of people to see the void and attempt to bridge the gap"!!

    Micah Vandegrift
    Scholarly Communications Librarian
