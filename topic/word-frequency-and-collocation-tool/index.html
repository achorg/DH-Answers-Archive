    I'm looking for a tool or set of tools that allow, as simply and easily as possible, for the following kinds of word frequency tests to be performed on a relatively large set of textual data: (i) ranked quantification of most frequently used words in a textual archive; (ii) plotting such things over time (i.e., how word frequencies rise and fall over time, as the dates of the individual textual items in the archive progress); and most importantly (iii) collocation tests identifying and ranking most frequently used words within, say, 25 or 50 words, of a target word specified within the textual archive (e.g., what's the most frequently used word with 25 words of "Tolstoy"?)

    Thanks much for any help anyone can provide.

  3. devinhiggins

    Based on my own very recent experience, I would also recommend the Natural Language Toolkit (NLTK) for Python. NLTK would make tasks (i) and (iii) quite simple, and I don't think task (ii) would be too much of a challenge either. If you have any familiarity with Python, NLTK would be an easy solution to implement, and the documentation in the form of this free e-book is great. You could also use NLTK to run MALLET topic modeling if so desired.

    @Jarom: in lleu of proper documentation (ahem), let me just say that the default ordering of collocates in Voyant is by its relative frequency "ratio", which is essentially the difference between the term's relative frequency within the contexts (text segments in proximity to the keyword) and its relative frequency in the entire document. That value is available in the "ratio" column of the collocates table, as in this example.
