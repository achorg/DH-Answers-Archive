    This might not be what you're looking for, but I think two of the most important (and better written) reads on Open Source are
    Eric Raymond's The Cathedral and the Bazaar and Yochai Benkler's "Coase's Penguin". The first looks at the ethos (and genesis) of open source software design and the later is a discussion of the economics of Open Source. Both have been around for a while, and while neither addresses the humanities is particular, I think the parallels are more than apparent.
