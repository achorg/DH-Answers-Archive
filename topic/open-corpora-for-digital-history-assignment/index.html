  4. SharonHoward

    Best Answer

    Lists of corpora compiled by historical linguists (some may be restricted access): http://www.helsinki.fi/varieng/corpora/index.html

    Oxford Texts Archive is full of amazing things (including EEBO-TCP texts), though students might have to register for access: http://ota.ox.ac.uk/

    This huge list of museums/libraries etc data compiled by Mia Ridge emphasises APIs and linked open data but it's actually very wide-ranging: http://museum-api.pbworks.com/w/page/21933420/Museum%C2%A0APIs

    The British Library is also making a varied assortment of datasets and text collections available through its BL Labs project: http://labs.bl.uk/Digital+Collections

    Corpora from Mark Davies, BYU (including Hansard): http://corpus.byu.edu/

    Some smaller collections that I like:

    Thomas Bodley Correspondence (XML) from the Centre for Lives and Letters: https://github.com/livesandletters/bodley1

    Family Life and Work Experience Before 1918, 1870-1973 (transcripts of c. 450 oral history interviews + structured metadata): https://discover.ukdataservice.ac.uk/catalogue/?sn=2000&type=Data%20catalogue

    Repository of Early American Datasets: http://repository.upenn.edu/mead/

    A new version of the Old Bailey Online Corpus is due to be released shortly and will be open data (the current version requires registration): http://www1.uni-giessen.de/oldbaileycorpus/
