    Replying to @MelissaTerras's post:

    Hey, Melissa, I've been waiting to see if anybody would answer this question and thought I'd give it a little *bump* even though I don't have a brillant example to share.

    I can say that we considered creating an online, interactive version of our scanning helpsheet at the Scholars' Lab (here it is, in PDF format) and ultimately decided -- since software gets updated so frequently and, for our local needs, people seemed to prefer keeping a hard copy at hand -- that it'd be less hassle just to produce a simple, printable version.

    Of course, our helpsheet is just about the scanning process. UVa Library also has a LibGuide for our community digitization standards -- minimal requirements for deposit of digitized text, audio, and image, and accomanying metadata. But this, too, isn't terribly interactive.

    I'd be interested to hear what others are doing, or if anybody knows of an online tutorial like you describe!
