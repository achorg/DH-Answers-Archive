  4. Matthew Wilkens
    Member

    In my own work with c. 1100 volumes of nineteenth-century fiction supplied by Indiana (an expanded set of what's in MONK), I've found that there are only about 50 unique publication locations in the metadata. It's not what you're looking for, but that's a small enough set that you could geocode them by hand. Place of composition, though, isn't addressed. FWIW (and again, totally not your question), there's some information about extracting named places from these texts on my blog.

    Take a look at the Neue Deutsche Biographie (http://data.deutsche-biographie.de/about/) and TELOTA (http://pdr.bbaw.de/english/). Both are biography, not bibliography projects but focus on literary figures etc. and do include bibliographic and location data on their subjects.
