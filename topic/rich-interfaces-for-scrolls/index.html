    In surveying for existing web/mobile interfaces for digitized scrolls or scroll paintings, I'm mostly seeing fairly straightforward examples, interface-wise. I'm not finding many with rich annotation built in, for example, to say nothing of user-contributed content. Almost all are Flash-based enlargements with, in some cases, accompanying text and/or media. Strangely enough, I'm also finding very few of recent vintage. Are there any good examples out there of sophisticated UI/UX work with horizontal scrolls?

    A handful of examples of what I'm finding:
    http://lucian.uchicago.edu/blogs/caea/scrolling-paintings/ (good for comparison tool)
    http://library.brown.edu/cds/perry/scroll_bul.html (good for paratext)
    http://ocw.mit.edu/ans7870/21f/21f.027/black_ships_and_samurai_02/scroll_home.html
    http://etcweb.princeton.edu/asianart/interactives/koetsu/scroll.html (starting to get there)
    http://www.courses.fas.harvard.edu/~icgzmod/qingming_student.html (closest I've found to what I would like to do)
    http://learn.bowdoin.edu/heijiscroll/viewer.html (also very good)
    http://dss.collections.imj.org.il/isaiah (also good)
    http://academic.reed.edu/hellscrolls/scrolls/Aseries/A01/index.html (a little naive but some good ideas)

    Right.. and several of these are based on Zoomify. Years (and years..) ago I experimented with QTVR to achieve a similar effect. You can see a demo here (requires QuickTime Player): http://dl.dropbox.com/u/1667511/frieze-web.mov The Mac app I used to create this no longer exists (I forget the name now..) though I'm pretty sure you'd be able to re-create this VR based technique using e.g. http://panotools.sourceforge.net/

    The only interesting, perhaps comparable and relevant work I've seen recently that might have bearing here is derived from CSS3 and Canvas experiments. You can find these on various sites which showcase HTML5 & Canvas.

    These days with hardware assisted support for WebGL already in place on modern browsers and touch, swipe & pinch gestures becoming ubiquitous even on desktops & laptops I think drawing on these kinds of paradigms plus different ways to represent overlaid information might be the way to go. One obvious place to look, for example, is how different apps are handling this kind of interactivity for photos and maps. The ability to use gestures on images to trigger "forensic" (analytic) changes no longer requires something like Shockwave for example.

    Please do post back here when you come across more examples. I would imagine that coming up with a good UI, backed by open technology standards (i.e. no more plugins..) would be something that could easily receive funding. Lots of people need something like this.
