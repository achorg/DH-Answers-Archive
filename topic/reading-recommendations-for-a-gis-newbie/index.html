    Hi Eric

    I'd recommend the following books and taking a look at the bibliography and resources pages on the HGIS website

    http://www.hgis.org.uk/bibliography.htm

    http://www.hgis.org.uk/resources.htm

    Gregory, Ian N. and Paul S. Ell. Historical GIS: Technologies, Methodologies, and Scholarship. Cambridge Studies in Historical Geography. 2007

    Sinton, Diana Stuart and Jennifer J. Lund Understanding Place: GIS and Mapping Across the Curriculm ESRI Press, 2006.

    Also though I haven't had a chance to read it yet, I think the following book may also be of interest

    Bodenhamer, David J. et al The Spatial Humanities: GIS and the Future of Humanities Scholarship Indiana U. Press, 2010

    And a GIS newbie in the digital humanities might want to start here:
    http://spatial.scholarslab.org/

    It includes some great essays by Jo Guldi framing the "spatial turn" across the disciplines, and a nascent, peer-reviewed set of step-by-step tutorials -- as well as a variety of crowdsourced resources drawn from Zotero groups, hashtags, and sites like... DH Answers! :)
