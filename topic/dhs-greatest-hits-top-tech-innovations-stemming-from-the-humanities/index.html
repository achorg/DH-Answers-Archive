    Just running through a few while I wait for the SWTOR queue. :)

    * RDF (Though the whole stack is still over complex)
    * Natural Language Toolkit (Python NLTK) and other NLP engines.
    * OAI-PMH
    * Big Social Media APIs (Twitter and Facebook primarily)
    * The I-don't-care-about-my-privacy mavens brave enough to post all their personal details.
    * I think MongoDB will be big or useful but nobody is really leveraging it yet.
    * Government supported Open Data initiatives. (http://services.data.gov/sparql) Europe is way ahead of the US in this regard and will likely stay there for some time.
    * SPARQL querying language overall. (Noticing a trend here?)
    * On Demand cloud services like Rackspace that put larger server resources in DH hands on an as-needed basis.

    to expand re metadata: looking for patterns in culture, productions of culture, items of culture, etc., is a fundamentally humanistic enterprise, as is creating ways to attach words or codes to those patterns in order to sort and categorize and pattern them some more.

    and, in the same way, i would argue that object-oriented approaches to computing are also based in a (sometimes overly simplified) humanistic understanding of a post-renaissance western world. so that's another one to look at.
