    A few years ago there was some buzz around the idea that Vermeer and other Dutch artists used a camera obscura to help draft their paintings. Philip Steadman from.. err, University College, London wrote a book about this so you must know all about it already. Well, anyway, maybe some others haven't heard of it yet so I'll leave the reference. See http://www.amazon.com/Vermeers-Camera-Uncovering-Behind-Masterpieces/dp/0192159674 and http://www.vermeerscamera.co.uk/

    I've not read it myself and so I can't comment to what extent he carried out computer analysis per se but I know a lot of effort was put into 3D reconstructions of the spaces in the paintings.

    There's a whole sub-field in computer aided visualization that deals with extracting 3D info from 2D image sources. See, for example, http://ict.debevec.org/~debevec/Research/ I would look through last few SIGGRAPH Proceedings. That's where the best of this kind of work gets presented and published.

    For an informed art historian's view on this I'd drop a line to Barbara Stafford formerly at the University of Chicago (now retired). She's still very active in the field, publishing and lecturing, and would very likely know who is working on these kinds of approaches. http://barbaramariastafford.com/
