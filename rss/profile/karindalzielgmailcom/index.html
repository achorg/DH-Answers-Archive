Something I realized immediately was that it's fairly counterproductive to have non-native English speakers (say, the project's developers) create an initial "literal" translation and then request a native speaker to add some final "polish" (no, not Polish!) to the final draft. Untangling the resulting mangle of embedded idioms is extremely time consuming and fraught with compromises. You'd be much better off re-writing the manual from scratch. The better approach, I feel, is to develop a well thought out style manual and high-level concept for the translation, ask the developers to deliver a combination of terse, bullet-point style lists and longer explanations where necessary describing the functions and then have the translation written by a native speaker.
