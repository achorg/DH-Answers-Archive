This has a couple of nice side-benefits: first, it'll get their material into a format that they can footnote/cite (Omeka creates the citation link) and along the way force them to tighten up their record-keeping a little. Two, it gives them a standard way to relate items to each other (either via the DC Relation field, or if they're meticulous, via FRBR) in an organized and consistent manner. Third, all metadata is searchable by field and as full-text. This already covers much of what they would want from a locally stored and cataloged collection. Granted this will require a greater degree of organization and discipline up front but it'll pay off dividends later on by attracting others interested in the topic and by generating some early recognition for their expertise. </p> <p>For a very nice example of this type of set-up, see Radu Suciu's online archive <a href="http://melancholystories.com">Medical Case Studies on Renaissance
