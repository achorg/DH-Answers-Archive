Make sure that the problem you're addressing hasn't already been solved by someone else (e.g., demonstrate knowledge of relevant literature), and make sure that you clearly explain the importance of addressing it, as well as the promise of your approach. </p> <p>* Make sure everything in your budget is accounted for in your proposal, and (even more important) make sure that any actual work you propose to do is clearly represented in your budget. </p> <p>* Obey grant guidelines, especially with respect to requirements and limitations. </p> <p>* Understand the goals of the funder in the grant program to which you're applying, and explain how the work you want to do connects with and advances those goals. </p> <p>The most common failings of bad proposals, in my experience, are failures to observe one or more of those rules.
