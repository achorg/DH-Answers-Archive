There was recently a paper on automatic classification of Australian rock art at CAA, but I've seen other papers on classification of Roman coins (based on both 2D images and 3D scans) and laser scanned 3D objects, like the Forma Urbis project. I think there are a lot of avenues for AI in art history. Classification of objects is an often time-consuming task that could be made much faster by automated processes, for example, categorization of Greek pottery based on styles of drawing. I'm frankly not very sure you can accomplish writing sophisticated algorithms for this sort of problem in six months, but you could have a go at it.
