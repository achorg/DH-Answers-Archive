I am also a big fan of her article "Wampum as Hypertext: An American Indian Intellectual Tradition of Multimedia Theory and Practice."</p> <p>Melanie Yergeau is doing really interesting stuff drawing from disability studies on autism and multimodality. I'm not sure what she's published on since I gather this from a conference presentation I saw earlier this year. I wrote a bit about it on the CHI blog, though my description probably really oversimplifies her presentation (see the last couple paragraphs): <a href="http://chi.matrix.msu.edu/2011/05/06/digital-rhetorics-at-cccc-2011/" rel="nofollow">http://chi.matrix.msu.edu/2011/05/06/digital-rhetorics-at-cccc-2011/</a> She also has a webtext, "aut(hored)ism" published through Computers &amp; Composition Online here: <a href="http://www.bgsu.edu/cconline/dmac/index.html#" rel="nofollow">http://www.bgsu.edu/cconline/dmac/index.html#</a></p> <p>Iswari Pandey's "Literate Lives Across the Digital Divide," published in Computers &amp; Composition, traces the writer's movement between Nepal and the U.S. over time and talks about the relationship between technology and politics across these spaces. He argues that while we often think of access as contingent on things like wealth and status, governmental politics also play a huge role.</p> <p>Doug Walls has a *fantastic* webtext published in Kairos called "An 'A' word production: A case for mindful rhetorical media use" <a href="http://kairos.technorhetoric.net/13.1/disputatio/walls/" rel="nofollow">http://kairos.technorhetoric.net/13.1/disputatio/walls/</a></p> <p>Robyn Tasaka wrote a dissertation that explores racial and ethnic representation on college Hawai'i club websites, titled, "Challenges and privileges, entanglement and appropriation: Rhetorical practices of Asian Americans from Hawai'i"</p> <p>Other stuff I've come across but not gotten around to reading myself:<br /> Cameron Bailey's "Virtual Skin: Articulating Race in Cyberspace"<br /> Kristine Blair &amp; Pamela Takayoshi's collection Feminist Cyberscapes: Mapping Gendered Academic Spaces<br /> Byron Burkhalter's "Reading Race Online: Discovering Racial Identity in Usenet Discussions"<br /> Radhika Gajjala's Cyberselves: Feminist Ethnographies of South Asian Women<br /> Emily Ignacio's Building Diaspora: Filipino Cultural Community Formation on the Internet<br /> Lena Karlsson's “Consuming Lives, Creating Community: Female Chinese-American Diary Writing on the Web”<br /> Lori Kendall's "Class, Race, and Online Participation"<br /> Stephen Knadler's “E-Racing Difference in E-Space: Black Female Subjectivity and the Web-Based Portfolio”<br /> Beth Kolko, Lisa Nakamura, and Gilbert Rodman's collection, Race in Cyberspace<br /> Linda Leung's “Where Am I and Who Are ‘We’?: Self-representation and the Intersection of Gender and Ethnicity on the Web”<br /> Heidi McKee's “‘YOUR VIEWS SHOWED TRUE IGNORANCE!!!’: (Mis)Communication in an Online Interrracial Discussion Forum”<br /> Steven McLaine's “Ethnic Online Communities: Between Profit and Purpose”<br /> Ananda Mitra's “Virtual Commonality: Looking for India on the Internet”<br /> Alondra Nelson &amp; Thuy Linh Tu's collection Technicolor: Race, Technology, and Everyday Life<br /> Susanna Paasonen's “The Woman Question: Addressing Women as Internet Users”<br /> Dora Ramírez Dhoore's "The Cyberborderland: Surfing the Web for Xicanidad" </p> James Neal on "Where might I find resources and literature regarding diversity and DH?" http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/topic/where-might-i-find-resources-and-literature-regarding-diversity-and-dh#post-1200 Thu, 09 Jun 2011 09:19:02 +0000 James Neal 1200@http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/ <p>Anything and everything ... </p> Jennifer Sano-Franchini on "Where might I find resources and literature regarding diversity and DH?" http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/topic/where-might-i-find-resources-and-literature-regarding-diversity-and-dh#post-1199 Thu, 09 Jun 2011 04:22:08 +0000 Jennifer Sano-Franchini 1199@http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/ <p>Just for clarification--are you looking for works that talk about diversity, inclusion, and access within DH as a field of study (i.e., works that discuss the backgrounds of folks who identify as DHists and the work that they do)?
