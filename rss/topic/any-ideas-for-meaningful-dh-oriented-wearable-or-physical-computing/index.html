Most of them did a final project together which involved building on the Monome -&gt; Arduinome. They basically developed a basic platform for experimenting with interactivity built on the whole Arduinome design and then illustrated a number of different things that could be done with this platform. I was sufficiently impressed that I have commissioned two of the boxes (one nice touch - they designed an enclosure for the platform) so that we can begin a series of experiments in public interactivity.</p> <p>Another student in the reading course created a jacket that could be used as a prop for magic shows. He is a performing magician - his jacket has lights under the fabric that he could trigger by playing with a jacket button. The light sequences were designed to add to his performances. </p> <p>I don't know if these ideas are useful, and I would love to have you involved in the interactivity project.</p> <p>One thing we did find in the course was that:</p> <p>a) there was a real difference between how MA students do research and how MDes students do it. </p> <p>b) the design students are used to iteratively sketching ideas in a way that got the students unstuck. </p> <p>So, I guess I'm recommending that you talk to some design folk about how they think about design.
