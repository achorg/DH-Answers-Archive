Dorothea's advice about context(s) and projects is super helpful here. But, since you're asking about learning it, chances are it might be hard to respond to that question, huh? :)</p> <p>To me, the first issue is that "XML" is sort of a meta-language. Very loosely said, it's a set of rules and principles that are used to create particular vocabularies (schema) for particular needs/types of projects. You'll see that in the acronym-soup coming up!</p> <p>But there are, again very generally, two types of schema: document-centric and data-centric.</p> <p>Document-centric schema are things like XHTML or TEI. If your project/interests are along those lines, I'd second Doug's links, and also suggest looking at the <a href="http://www.oasis-open.org/docbook/documentation/reference/html/docbook.html">DocBook</a> intro. DocBook is also for documents, but more tailored to documentation. I think a lot of the DocBook examples made more intuitive sense to me when I was learning.</p> <p>Data-centric schema are more like MODS or OAI-PMH, or even RSS or the KML for GoogleEarth, and also cover more technical data-exchange over the web. The <a href="http://dublincore.org/documents/dc-xml-guidelines">examples of using XML for Dublin Core</a> might be a starting point for that angle, and I'm guessing you might have some metadata around that you could practice with! :)</p> <p>Last, and maybe most important, I'd suggest you get the trial version of <a href="http://oxygenxml.com/">oXygen XML editor</a> to start playing with and writing XML. The UI will be a bit much at first, but if you close all but the main panel and fire up a new XML document, it will offer lots of helpful tips for the hands-on approach. </p> Douglas Knox on "Advice for teaching myself XML?" http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/topic/advice-for-teaching-myself-xml#post-922 Wed, 26 Jan 2011 00:22:24 +0000 Douglas Knox 922@http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/ <p>Whether or not you are interested in TEI specifically, the <a href="http://www.tei-c.org/release/doc/tei-p5-doc/en/html/SG.html">Gentle Introduction to XML</a> at the beginning of the TEI guidelines is well worth reading and bookmarking, particularly when you want to become familiar with some of the more advanced syntax details without going to the formal spec. </p> <p>If you are interested in TEI, you might also look at <a href="http://tbe.kantl.be/TBE/TBE.htm">TEI By Example,</a> which has some hands-on tutorials and exercises. </p> Dorothea Salo on "Advice for teaching myself XML?" http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/topic/advice-for-teaching-myself-xml#post-921 Tue, 25 Jan 2011 21:56:45 +0000 Dorothea Salo 921@http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/ <p>Could you tell us a little more about the context(s) you want to use XML in?
