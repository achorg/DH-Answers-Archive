Even though I was trying to put the brakes on, I'm so much a first son that I have my own ideologies about formats, and those probably shine through quite clearly :-).</p> <p>Trying for a better approach: it seems to me that most of the DH-related data curation I've been involved with has revolved around migration/rescue. That is, the conversion from either an obsolete format (e.g. SGML) to a modern one (e.g. XML), or from one type of encoding to another (ISO 8859-1 to UTF-8), or extracting data from some system (perhaps a desktop db like Filemaker or Access) for insertion into another (say MySQL). That seems like a core task that isn't going away. It's made difficult when I have to ask questions or make assumptions about the intentions of the data's creators: "What does this mean?"; "How are these things related"; "What's missing?"</p> <p>It's harder still when there's nobody to ask those questions of, or when there are layers of different decisions by different people in the data.</p> <p>I think documentation and metadata are things that can help mitigate the pain of those who will have to deal with your data in its afterlife. Open formats and the use of open source are good in this context because they are more likely to be available and usable (or able to be modified so that they can be used, or at the very least able to be read so they can be re-created).</p> <p>Update/postscript: put another way, participants in DH projects should imagine they are in a conversation with future scholars, and should explain not just what was done, but why and how. </p> Dorothea Salo on "Experience to share on DH data curation, FOSS/OA advocacy?" http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/topic/experience-to-share-on-dh-data-curation-fossoa-advocacy#post-801 Tue, 07 Dec 2010 19:51:00 +0000 Dorothea Salo 801@http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/ <p>I've put in my two cents at Book of Trogool: <a href="http://scientopia.org/blogs/bookoftrogool/2010/12/07/the-four-sons-of-digital-curation/">the Four Sons of digital curation</a>. </p> Hugh Cayless on "Experience to share on DH data curation, FOSS/OA advocacy?" http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/topic/experience-to-share-on-dh-data-curation-fossoa-advocacy#post-800 Mon, 06 Dec 2010 20:58:41 +0000 Hugh Cayless 800@http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/ <p>First (though I'm sure you've looked at it already) this thread: <a href="http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/topic/what-does-it-mean-to-future-proof-a-dh-project" rel="nofollow">http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/topic/what-does-it-mean-to-future-proof-a-dh-project</a> has some good stuff in it (even my comment).</p> <p>Second: the anti/pro embedded markup (read TEI) stuff is a rat-hole.* Avoid it. But there's absolutely nothing wrong with plain text. Pick the best tool for your task and move on.</p> <p>The data curation task is enabled by careful thought and planning around data format decisions, good documentation of the practices used in creating the data and the recording of information about its state and how it got there. </p> <p>*A rabbit hole is a distracting tangent to the discussion that might have something good as an outcome. To correct this you need to write a program and to have a parser handy.
