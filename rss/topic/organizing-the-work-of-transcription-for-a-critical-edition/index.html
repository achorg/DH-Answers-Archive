As you've noticed it has evolved and can now read TEI. I don't know what are your mark-up practices, but the ability to only compare the tags that you choose might be what you are looking for when you talk about comparing specific lemmata. Juxta is also still in continuous development, so stay tuned. I foresee Juxta giving back the editor even more control over what to collate and what not collate.</p> <p>b) I don't recommend tagging from the beginning, since once the transcription is done, with some of the software we have out there it is easy to "wrap" blocks of text with the tags you need. You do want to leave markers for yourself as you go along, though. For example, if I'm transcribing prose, I leave myself a simple paragraph break marker, /p, as I go along. Notice that actual characters of your markers don't need to be proper XML or HTML or what have you. They just need to be easy to type, unique things that you can Find and Replace when it comes time to marking-up. In this case, after I am done I do a find and replace that looks something like this: Find: <code>/p</code> ; Replace with: <code>&lt;/p&gt;&lt;p&gt;</code></p> <p>c) Have you heard of <a href="http://scripto.org/">Scripto?</a> Perhaps this would move things faster for you. </p> Carin Ruff on "Organizing the work of transcription for a critical edition" http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/topic/organizing-the-work-of-transcription-for-a-critical-edition#post-871 Mon, 17 Jan 2011 17:56:08 +0000 Carin Ruff 871@http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/ <p><em>Replying to @<a href='/DH-Answers-Archive/profile/rlee13'>rlee13</a>'s <a href="http://digitalhumanities.org/answers/topic/organizing-the-work-of-transcription-for-a-critical-edition#post-865">post</a>:</em></p> <p>Thanks, Rachel.
